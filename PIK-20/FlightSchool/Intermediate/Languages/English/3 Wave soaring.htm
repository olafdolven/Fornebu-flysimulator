Wave soaring

Wave lift can be found in special conditions at the lee side of the mountain ridges.

If the wind is blowing perpendicular to a long ridge, then on the front side of the ridge the air will rise and normal ridge lift can be expected. The air will then sink at the lee side. If the atmosphere is very stable and the wind is strong, the air will rebound upward once again. This upward swing is called wave lift. With strong wind and stable atmosphere, there are multiple waves, however, usually the first is the strongest.

Just like with the cumulus clouds caused by air rising, there are wave cloud indicators – lenticularis clouds. Depending on humidity of upper layers of atmosphere, they can, but not have to appear. Lenticularis clouds form at the top of the wave, so their upwind side is located in the lift, and lee side in the sink area.

Those lift and sink areas are much bigger than ridge and thermal ones, keep it in mind, that flying against the wind of sometimes even 100kmh and entering sink area can be dangerous. Reserve altitude or plan B where to retreat is key factor when flying between waves.
